Ingredients:
  1. 1 pound pre-tenderized cube steaks, or round steaks (4 steaks)
  2. Kosher salt, for seasoning the meat
  3. 2 large eggs, beaten
  4. 1/2 cup milk
  5. 2 cups all-purpose flour
  6. 2 teaspoons kosher salt, for breading
  7. 1 1/2 teaspoons garlic powder
  8. 1/2 teaspoon cayenne
  9. Canola oil, peanut oil, olive oil, or other fat for frying
    10. For the gravy:
  10. 3 tablespoons pan drippings
  11. 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  12. 1/4 cup heavy cream
  13. 1 3/4 cups milk (a little more or less, depending on your preference)
  14. Kosher salt, to taste
  15. Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

For the steak:
  1. Begin with setting up an assembly line of dishes. Beat the milk with the eggs in one; the flour mixed with the seasoned salt, 1 1/2 teaspoons black pepper, paprika and the end to receive the breaded meat.
  2. Work with one piece of meat at a time. Sprinkle both sides with. Place the breaded meat on the clean plate.
  3. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the butter. you know it's ready.
    4. For the gravy:
  4. When the grease is hot, sprinkle the flour evenly over the grease. Using a whisk, mix the flour with the grease, creating a golden-brown paste. Add more flour if it looks overly greasy; add a little more grease if it becomes too pasty/clumpy. Keep cooking until the roux reaches a deep golden brown color.
  5. Serve the meat next to a big side of mashed potatoes. Pour gravy over the whole shebang!

Ingredients:

  1. 2 large skinless boneless chicken breasts pounded to 1-inch thickness (or 4 boneless and skinless chicken thigh fillets) Salt and pepper, to season
  2. 1/2 teaspoon paprika (sweet or smokey)
  3. 1/2 teaspoon dried parsley
  4. 1 tablespoon oil, divided (use olive or canola oil)
  5. 2 tablespoons butter
  6. 1 small yellow onion chopped
  7. 6 cloves garlic finely diced
  8. 1/3 cup white wine OPTIONAL (use chicken broth instead if you wish)
  9. 9 oz (250g) jarred sun dried tomato strips in oil (reserve 2 tablespoons of oil and drain the rest)
  10. 3 level tablespoons flour
  11. 2 cups chicken broth
  12. 3 cups milk OR light cream* or half and half, divided
  13. 2 teaspoons dried Italian herbs
  14. 10 ounces (300g) elbow macaroni uncooked (3 cups!)

Instructions:

  1. Season chicken with salt, pepper, paprika, dried parsley and 2 teaspoons of the oil. Heat the remaining oil in a large (30cm or 12-inch) pot or pan over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and sear on both sides until golden brown, cooked through and no longer pink in the middle. Transfer chicken to a warm plate, tent with foil and set aside.
  2. To the same pan, add the butter and fry the onion and garlic until the onion becomes transparent, stirring occasionally (about 2 minutes). Pour in the white wine and allow to simmer for 5 minutes, or until beginning to reduce down.
  3. Add the sun dried tomatoes with 2 tablespoons of the sun dried tomato oil from the jar and cook for 1-2 minutes to release as much flavour as possible.
Ingredients:
  1. Olive oil
  2. Boneless skinless chicken breasts
  3. 21 Salute Seasoning (or poultry seasoning)
  4. Salt
  5. Pepper
  6. White wine or chicken broth
  7. Butter
  8. Garlic
  9. Fresh parsley, optional

Instructions:

  1. To a large skillet, add olive oil, chicken breasts seasoned with 21 Salute Seasoning, salt, pepper, and sear on the first side for about 5 minutes.
  2. Flip chicken oven and cook on the second side for about 5 minutes.
  3. When the chicken is done and cooked through, remove it from the pan and allow it to rest on a plate.
  4. Deglaze your pan with a splash of wine, add the butter, garlic, and cook for one minute, or until the butter has melted and the garlic is fragrant.
  5. Return the chicken to the pan, toss it in the garlic butter sauce, garnish with parsley, and serve.
